How does Early Check test for these conditions?

Treatable Conditions [and Conditions with Potential Treatments]

Early Check uses DNA sequencing.

Most of the health conditions will be tested using a highly accurate test called DNA sequencing. This is spelling out and reading the baby’s genetic information (DNA).

The DNA sequencing is done by a partner lab called GeneDx that works with Early Check. Your baby’s blood sample would be sent to GeneDx to do the screening.

What type of results will I get for my baby?

Remember the goal of this screening test is to find serious health conditions in babies so they can get help sooner. There are two possible results reported from this screening:

  • Normal result
  • Not normal result

Most parents get a normal result for their baby. The Early Check screening tests are not perfect and will not find all health conditions in all babies.

Risk for type 1 diabetes (T1D)

DNA sequencing can also be used to check your baby’s risk for getting type 1 diabetes (T1D) during their lifetime. This screening for T1D looks for small differences in many genes, called markers. These markers can be a sign of a higher risk for T1D.

The more of these markers a baby has, the higher their chance of getting T1D during their lifetime.

What type of results will I get for my baby?

Remember, the goal of the screening is to estimate a baby’s risk for getting T1D during their lifetime. This is reported as a risk range. One of these three risk ranges will be reported from this screening:

  1. low risk,
  2. higher risk,
  3. highest risk

The Early Check screening for T1D does not give a yes or no answer about whether the baby will have T1D. This test cannot tell for sure if a baby will get T1D or at what age.
