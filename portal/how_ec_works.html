How does Early Check work?

Learn more about Early Check screening and how it is done.

A woman is pregnant, or a baby is born in North Carolina

  • New and expecting parents in North Carolina are invited to join Early Check, a research study that screens newborns for serious health conditions.

Parents choose Early Check

  • Choosing Early Check gives parents information about the health of their baby.
  • Doctors and researchers learn to find serious health conditions in babies and start helping them earlier.
  • Early Check is free and signing up is easy. Parents sign up their baby online. No appointment is needed.

Early Check does screening tests

  • Babies have a heel prick in the hospital for state newborn screening.
  • Early Check does extra screening tests on the same blood spot.
  • Most parents get Early Check initial screening results before the baby is 3-4 months old.

Parents get the Early Check results

  • Most parents get a normal result for their baby.

Normal results and risk for type 1 diabetes results (if parent chooses risk for T1D group)

  • An email tells parents that the Early Check test results are ready.
  • Parents view the results on the secure Early Check website.

Not normal result for screening groups 1 or 2

  • The Early Check team calls the parent(s)
  • A second test may be used to confirm the result of the first screening test.
  • The family gets information and genetic counseling.
  • Early Check helps find doctors to care for the baby and may provide an exam for babies with certain health conditions.

Researchers learn important information

  • Researchers learn about the screening tests, the screening process, and the health conditions to help other babies in the future.
